The Problem
Why the current process needs to change

The AIIA today relies on Excel files distributed by email — creating fragmentation, inconsistency, and zero oversight visibility across entities.

⚠ Today — Excel & Email Process
📎Assessments sent as spreadsheet attachments via email — no central record
🔀No dynamic routing — all entities fill the full questionnaire regardless of risk level
👥No structured handoff between focal points, contributors, and reviewers
📊Leadership cannot see risk distribution or coverage without manual aggregation
🔍No audit trail of who completed what, when, or what decisions were made
💬No way for entities to query guidance documents or prior decisions
🔄No lifecycle reassessment trigger when a use case evolves
✦ This Application — Digital Workflow
🌐Centralised online workflow — one place from registration to approval decision
🎯Screening routes each use case to minimal, standard, or full assessment automatically
🔐Role-based access with structured handoff, RBAC, and entity-scoped data isolation
📈Real-time dashboard with risk distribution, pipeline, and principle heatmap
📋Immutable audit log records every action — actor, timestamp, metadata
🤖Policy chat powered by RAG over approved guidance documents (RBAC-enforced)
⏱Questionnaire version tracking enables lifecycle reassessment when needed

Project Objectives
Nine goals driving this prototype
1
Digitise the AI Impact Assessment questionnaire
Extract questions, weights, options & principle mappings from source Excel into structured JSON and Supabase.
2
Dynamic screening and route selection
A short screener determines minimal, standard, or full assessment — reducing unnecessary burden.
3
Qualitative justification and evidence capture
Each response accepts free-text explanation and evidence URL to create a documented rationale for reviewers.
4
Preliminary risk scoring engine
Weighted question × response scores across 10 AI principles and 6 lifecycle stages → exposure and residual risk.
5
Mitigation action tracking
High-risk areas generate mitigation actions that entities document, track, and update over time.
6
Structured review and escalation workflow
Reviewers work a submission queue and record decisions: approve, return, request mitigation, or escalate.
7
Governance dashboards and analytics
Executive summary, risk distribution, principle heatmap, mitigation status — with role-scoped access.
8
Policy chat with RAG (retrieval-augmented generation)
Entities query guidance documents and prior decisions. RBAC enforces entity-scoped retrieval — no cross-entity leakage.
9
Azure migration-ready architecture
Clean adapter pattern allows migration to Azure PostgreSQL, Azure AI Search, and Microsoft Entra ID without rewriting core logic.

Role Deep-Dive · 1 of 4
Entity Focal Point — the assessment owner
🧑‍💼
Entity Focal Point
role: entity_focal_point
The designated governance officer within each participating UN entity. Owns the AI use case assessment lifecycle end-to-end — from registration to submission. One or more focal points may exist per entity.
Access & Capabilities
Register new AI use cases for their entity
Run screening questions and receive route assignment
Complete questionnaire sections (all principles + lifecycle stages)
Plan and document mitigation actions
Submit completed assessments to the reviewer queue
View entity dashboard — pipeline, risk, principle coverage
Query policy chat within entity scope
View own entity's audit log entries
Cannot see other entities' use cases, assessments, or scores
Cannot make review decisions or access the reviewer queue
Their Workflow Journey
1
Sign in & check dashboard
Entity scope, risk summary, and items needing action are visible immediately on login.
2
Register a new AI use case
Title, description, business problem, data types, lifecycle stage, and stakeholders.
3
Complete screening questions
Short screener routes the use case to Minimal / Standard / Full assessment automatically.
4
Fill questionnaire (+ assign to contributors)
Select responses, add evidence URLs and qualitative justifications per question.
5
Review preliminary risk score
Exposure and residual risk across 10 AI principles and 6 lifecycle stages.
6
Document mitigation actions
For each high-risk area, record what mitigation is in place or planned.
7
Submit for reviewer decision
Submission is recorded in the immutable audit log. Track the reviewer's decision in the dashboard.

Role Deep-Dive · 2 of 4
Contributor — the domain expert
🔬
Contributor
role: contributor
A technical or subject-matter expert within the same entity as the focal point. Assigned to complete specific questionnaire sections where their expertise applies — data protection, IT security, legal, programme, or risk management.
How they collaborate with the Focal Point
The Focal Point owns the use case and drives the overall assessment. The Contributor is a supporting expert who fills in specific sections that require technical or legal knowledge the focal point may not hold. Both work within the same entity scope — neither can see other entities.
Access & Capabilities
Complete assigned questionnaire sections with responses + justification
Add evidence URLs to support each response
Document mitigation actions for high-risk areas
View preliminary risk score for own entity's use cases
View entity dashboard (own entity only)
Query policy chat within entity scope
Cannot register new use cases
Cannot submit assessments for review
Cannot access review queue, analytics, or audit log
Cannot see any other entity's data
What the contributor sees in the app
📋 Assigned Questionnaire Sections
The contributor sees the full questionnaire for their entity's use cases. They select a response option from weighted choices, then add a free-text explanation and (optionally) an evidence URL for each question.
📊 Risk Score Preview (read-only)
Once questionnaire sections are completed, contributors can view the preliminary exposure score and residual risk breakdown across AI principles and lifecycle stages — but cannot change scoring logic.
🛡 Mitigation Actions
Contributors can draft and update mitigation actions for the areas they assessed. The focal point reviews and may edit these before submission.
Not visible to contributors: Review queue · Reviewer decisions · Cross-entity analytics · Audit trail · User management · Other entities' dashboards or assessments

Role Deep-Dive · 3 of 4
Reviewer — the cross-entity decision maker
⚖️
Reviewer
role: reviewer
The methodology owner or governance body member responsible for reviewing submitted assessments across all entities. Unlike entity roles, the reviewer sees all entities' submissions — the scope boundary is lifted for this role.
What the reviewer can access
Review queue — all entities' submitted assessments in one view
Full assessment detail: responses, justifications, evidence links
Preliminary risk score, principle heatmap, lifecycle risk breakdown
Mitigation actions documented by the entity
Record a structured decision with written rationale
Cross-entity analytics dashboard and principle coverage
Full audit trail across all entities
Policy chat with cross-entity RAG scope
Cannot register or complete use case assessments
Cannot manage users, entities, or questionnaire versions
Review Decision Tree
Assessment submitted to reviewer queue
Reviewer opens the full assessment — responses, scores, mitigation actions, evidence links
Is the assessment sufficiently complete and the risk acceptably managed?
✓ Approve
Risk score acceptable. Mitigation plan complete. Use case approved for deployment.
↩ Return for Revision
Assessment incomplete or rationale insufficient. Entity must revise and resubmit.
🛡 Request Mitigation
Risk score too high. Entity must strengthen mitigation plan before re-review.
⚠ Escalate
Very high risk or unresolvable concern. Referred to senior governance body.
Every decision is logged: The reviewer's decision, rationale text, and timestamp are appended to the immutable audit log. The entity focal point sees the outcome in their dashboard immediately.

Application Walkthrough
Step-by-step guide for entity focal points
1
Sign in at the login page
Use your assigned account or click "Continue with demo session". Role and entity are set on sign-in.
2
Check your dashboard
Your entity's use cases, risk scores, assessment pipeline, and any items needing action appear here.
3
Register a new AI use case
Navigate to Use Cases → New. Enter title, description, business problem, stakeholders, and lifecycle stage.
4
Complete the screening questions
Answer the short screener. The system routes you to Minimal, Standard, or Full assessment automatically.
5
Complete questionnaire sections
Select a response option per question. Add a qualitative explanation and evidence URL for each response.
6
Review your preliminary risk score
The system shows exposure and residual risk across principles and lifecycle stages. Review the risk category.
7
Add mitigation actions
For each area of concern, document the mitigation in place or planned. This reduces the residual risk score.
8
Submit for reviewer decision
When complete, submit. An audit log entry records the submission. Track the decision in your dashboard.
